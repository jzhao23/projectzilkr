STEP 1: TEAM BUILDING

The team behind a startup is undoubtedly the most important factor. We don’t advise that you attempt this process alone, entrepreneurship is a difficult journey and you have a great advantage if you have a solid team on your side. Here we’ll show you how to build a team with complementary skills and how to ensure your goals are aligned so you can work cohesively. We’ll also give you tips on how to work well in a team by establishing norms and a company culture.

Finding like minded people.

When building a team, you need to consider the problem you are solving because it's important that every team member is dedicated to finding the solution. Make sure that everyone's goals are aligned so that there's no miscommunications and people working at cross-purposes. A good way of doing this is to sit down together and discuss each of your values and your 'end game' - what you all hope to get out of this experience. If they are radically different, maybe you should reconsider going into business with that person.

Assigning Roles.

It’s important not to overwhelm yourself with any amount of work, and distribute the workload among your team. Take into account the time everyone has available when distributing roles, as well as their interests and skills. Roles can provide structure but remember you can lean on your team for support. There’s a few roles every start-up needs.

Project Manager

Organised people are best for this. They keep the boat sailing smoothly and coordinate team members and activites.

Marketer

Advertising and social media skills. Identifying your market and helping your bring your product to their hands

Tester

Messes around on the site. Infuriates everyone by finding flaws in the product. Remember, it’s better a member of your team finds flaws than the consumer.

Visionary

General idea shaper. Market fit and researcher.

Lead Developers

The programmers. The use web framework, coding knowledge is essential.

Graphic Artist

Colour, design, logos, icons and any images you may need come back to the artistically skilled amongst us. These people create your brand and your identity. Graphic designers can be outsourced, but it is always a plus to have someone who will work with you throughout the development of your ideas.


Don’t worry if you’re not sure where everyone fits just yet - take some time to see what each member of your team is good at and what he or she enjoys doing. Don’t ever try to fit someone into a position they’re uncomfortable with!

NOTE: Be careful when assigning your roles and take into account everything you may need. No team member is more important than another, you’re working together here. The best working teams don’t need a hierarchy to function.
