Our Curriculum

Hello! Welcome to the future home of the most progressive curriculum for young entrepreneurs ever compiled online.

Our 9 Step Process

Teaching materials on the site will cover these steps, and much more.

Find Your Ache

The team behind a startup is undoubtedly the most important factor. We’ll show you how to build a team with complimentary skills and how to ensure your goals are aligned so you can work cohesively.

Persona

At this stage we will show you how to pick out the perfect representative of your customer base. When finalizing details about your offering, you can keep this person in mind, asking questions like “What would Bob want?”

Revenue and Pricing

Time to look at all the ways your product could possibly make money. Pricing is a bit more technical than just sticking on a price tag. You have to take other factors into account - but don't worry, we'll help you.

Market Segmentation

Here’s where you brainstorm all the possible uses of your idea, even the crazy stuff. Then you can start narrowing down to the market that suits you best based on opportunity, possible share and accessibility.

Minimum Viable Product

It’s time to create your Minimum Viable Product: a prototype using the least resources possible which solves the customers needs in the way originally intended. Then you need to bring it to your beachhead market and get their feedback.

Customer Aquisition

Your next step is to figure out how you’ll reach your customers, and in more detail than just 'advertising'. You need to create a marketing plan based on the best ways to reach your beachhead market.

Market Research

Now you need to examine the market you have chosen. This means conducting interviews with possible customers to define exactly what your offering to them will be. You also will scope out your competitors.

Analysis

Here is when you look back on your progress so far and think about a few details you need to progress, for example how the customer will acquire your product, the use case, and your target persona’s decision making process.

Finance and Capital

Time to start thinking about the $$$. We’ll show you examples of great pitches, and give you tips on how to spark interest in investors.

Ready to start?
